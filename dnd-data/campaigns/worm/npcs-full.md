# NPCs — Full Entries (Worm)

Read the relevant entry before voicing an NPC or making decisions for them. **DM reference — contains canonical spoilers.** Voices and secrets here drive continuity; the index in `npcs.md` is surface-only.

Entries are written to support the protagonist stepping into Taylor's structural role. Where canon tied a beat specifically to Taylor's bug-control, a note flags how to re-thread it to the new protagonist's power once their character file lands.

## The Undersiders (the protagonist's crew — core relationships)

### Tattletale (Lisa)
- **Power:** Thinker — inference engine. Give her a fact and she deduces a chain of hidden truths; she "just knows" things, at the cost of headaches when she pushes. Mechanically: extreme Insight/Investigation, reads people and situations cold.
- **Role:** The Undersiders' brain and the protagonist's first genuine ally. She recruits/welcomes the protagonist and sees through them immediately — including secrets they're keeping (e.g. an undercover motive). She keeps that secret, which is the foundation of their bond.
- **Personality:** Quick, teasing, deflects with smugness; underneath, fiercely loyal and lonelier than she lets on. Speech quirk: finishes your thought, answers the question you didn't ask.
- **Secret/goal:** Has her own reasons to resent the people pulling strings; plays a longer game than she shows. Knows who the patron is well before the team does.
- **Relationships:** Closest to the protagonist and to Grue; wary co-conspirator with Coil.

### Grue (Brian)
- **Power:** Shaker — generates a cloud of impenetrable darkness that muffles sound and senses (and dampens powers within it). Mechanically: area obscurement/control.
- **Role:** The team's de-facto leader and steady hand. Older, working to gain custody of his younger sister (Aisha/Imp) — his reason for the money.
- **Personality:** Calm, responsible, protective; the adult in the room. Speech quirk: low-key, asks if you're okay and means it.
- **Relationships:** Anchor of the team; potential romance/close trust with the protagonist (canon had this with Taylor — let it grow or not per play).

### Regent (Alec)
- **Power:** Master — hijacks others' bodies/nervous systems; subtle, can nudge or seize control of limbs. Mechanically: control/save-or-be-moved.
- **Role:** The detached one; treats everything as a game. Hides a dark past tied to a major villain (he's the estranged son of a Slaughterhouse-tier figure — keep this for a mid-game reveal).
- **Personality:** Deadpan, bored, casually amoral but oddly steady in a crisis. Speech quirk: flat one-liners.
- **Relationships:** Odd-couple partnership with Imp later; needles everyone, loyal in his way.

### Bitch / Hellhound (Rachel)
- **Power:** Master — transforms dogs into huge monstrous mounts (muscle, bone-spurs, size). Mechanically: summons heavy beast allies.
- **Role:** The hardest to reach. Reads social cues poorly, trusts dogs over people, tests newcomers with hostility. Earning her respect is a real arc.
- **Personality:** Blunt to the point of cruelty, literal, fiercely protective of her animals and — eventually — her pack of people. Speech quirk: terse, threats stated as facts.
- **Relationships:** Comes to count the protagonist as pack if they earn it (don't lie to her, don't pity her, stand your ground).

### Imp (Aisha)
- **Power:** Stranger — when active, no one can perceive or remember her. Mechanically: near-total stealth/forgettability.
- **Role:** Grue's younger sister; joins later. Brash teenager who weaponizes being overlooked.
- **Personality:** Mouthy, reckless, funny. Speech quirk: shows up mid-conversation having heard everything.

## Coil (the hidden patron → antagonist)
- **Power:** Thinker/precog (effectively) — experiences two simultaneous timelines and discards the worse one, so he never seems to lose. (His "split" is a one-shot-per-decision safety net; the truth of its limits matters late.)
- **Role:** Secretly bankrolls the Undersiders. Calm, methodical mercenary building a private army (his uniformed soldiers) to seize Brockton Bay as his personal fiefdom. Holds a captive precog (Dinah) to plan his takeover.
- **Personality:** Soft-spoken, controlled, treats people as assets and contingencies. Never raises his voice. Speech quirk: unfailingly polite while threatening you.
- **Secret/goal:** Owns the city by the numbers; the protagonist's crew are pieces in his plan. The turn against him is a central mid-game beat. **Dinah's captivity is the moral lever** that turns the protagonist against him.
- **Relationships:** Patron to the Undersiders (via Tattletale as liaison); Tattletale loathes and fears him.

## Dinah Alcott
- **Power:** Thinker — precognition as probability ("chance that X happens if I do Y" to many decimal places). A civilian child, not a fighter.
- **Role:** Coil's prisoner, kept drugged and used as a forecasting tool. Her rescue is a moral throughline for the protagonist.
- **Personality:** Frightened, exhausted, painfully precise; speaks in percentages.

## The Protectorate & Wards (the heroes the protagonist didn't join)

### Armsmaster (Colin)
- **Power:** Tinker — efficiency specialist; his halberd packs dozens of functions. Mechanically: artificer with one extraordinary signature weapon.
- **Role:** Local Protectorate leader. Competent, driven, willing to bend rules for results — which gets him in trouble around the Leviathan fight. A foil for the protagonist's own pragmatism.
- **Personality:** Blunt, awkward, mission-focused; poor at people, good at problems. Later reinvents himself (as Defiant).

### Miss Militia (Hannah)
- **Power:** Blaster — conjures any weapon she's familiar with as a glowing energy form; never sleeps. Mechanically: infinite armory, always armed.
- **Role:** The Protectorate's warm, principled second; effectively its conscience. The most reasonable authority figure the protagonist can deal with.
- **Personality:** Kind but firm, soldier's discipline, carries old trauma quietly.

### The Wards
- **Clockblocker (Dennis):** Striker — freezes anything he touches in time (locked, invulnerable, for a duration he can't predict). Jokes constantly; smarter and sadder than the jokes suggest.
- **Vista:** Shaker — warps distance/space around herself; the most powerful Ward, and the youngest, and sick of being treated as a kid.
- **Gallant (Dean):** Master/Blaster — reads emotions as color, fires concussive emotion-blasts. Decent, gentle; dating Glory Girl.

### New Wave (independent heroes)
- **Glory Girl (Victoria):** Brute — flight, invulnerability, and an involuntary **aura of awe/fear**. Brash, larger-than-life, a little reckless with her power.
- **Panacea (Amy):** biokinetic — heals (or could do far worse) any living thing by touch, but can't touch brains by self-imposed rule. Crushingly overworked, guilt-ridden, quietly breaking under the weight of what she could do and won't. A tragic figure; handle with care.

## The gangs (early antagonists)

### Lung (ABB)
- **Power:** Brute/Changer — escalates continuously in a fight: tougher, larger, hotter, eventually a rage-dragon. The danger is *time* — beat him fast or not at all. Mechanically: scaling boss whose stats climb each round.
- **Role:** Rules the ABB by sheer threat. The protagonist's **inciting fight** — canon had Taylor neutralize him with a swarm exploiting his eyes/senses while he was distracted. **Re-thread:** the new protagonist's power should give them a clever, narrow win condition against an enemy they can't out-slug — design the "how" to their power.
- **Personality:** Speaks rarely; quiet pride and absolute confidence. Respects strength.

### Bakuda (ABB)
- **Power:** Tinker — bombs with impossible effects (time-stop, gravity, transmutation, pain). Mechanically: a Tinker whose every "spell" is a themed grenade.
- **Role:** Lung's lieutenant; when Lung is removed, she terrorizes the whole city with a bombing campaign and implanted bombs. Volatile, sadistic, brilliant.

### Empire 88 — Kaiser
- **Power:** Shaker — grows blades and spikes of metal from any surface. Mechanically: battlefield control + impalement.
- **Role:** Leader of the white-supremacist Empire 88, the city's biggest gang. Cold, patient, theatrical. Holds a stable of capes (Hookwolf, Krieg, Night & Fog, the twins, Purity in the past).
- **Purity (note):** former Empire Blaster (radiant light/flight) trying to go independent and be "good" while still a bigot — a study in self-deception.

### Merchants — Skidmark
- **Power:** Shaker — creates fields that violently accelerate anything crossing them. Foul-mouthed, twitchy, runs the drug-gang Merchants with his tinker partner Squealer. Desperate and dangerous in a mob.

## The escalating threats (mid-to-late)

### The Slaughterhouse Nine
- **Jack Slash:** Striker — extends his blades' cutting edge invisibly to range; his real weapon is *people* — manipulation, patience, and an uncanny knack for survival. The most dangerous man alive because of what he understands, not what he cuts. Tests "promising" capes and invites them to join by force.
- **Bonesaw (Riley):** bio-Tinker — a little girl who builds horrors out of flesh; sweet, polite, monstrous. A victim and a perpetrator at once.
- **The Siberian:** a silent, unstoppable striped woman who renders anything she touches; the truth of what she *is* is a key reveal (a projection, not the cape herself).
- **Use:** They arrive mid-campaign, mark the protagonist, and force impossible choices. They are a horror-movie, not a brawl.

### The Endbringers
- **Leviathan:** water and momentum; floods and drowns; attacks Brockton Bay in the campaign's first great catastrophe. A forced **Endbringer truce** has all capes fight side by side. Many die. The city is permanently broken.
- **The Simurgh:** the worst — a precognitive that "sets up" people and cities like dominoes; her victims are quarantined because they become time-bombs. Pure dread.
- **Behemoth:** the first and most directly destructive; a walking reactor.
- **Use:** Not negotiable, not killable (early). They reframe every rivalry the moment they appear.

### The Triumvirate (national heroes)
- **Alexandria (Rebecca):** flightless-rumored Brute + perfect-recall Thinker; iron-willed public face of heroism with a hidden Cauldron agenda. Pragmatic to ruthlessness.
- **Legend:** Blaster of staggering range/versatility; genuinely good, genuinely kind — and kept in the dark by his own colleagues.
- **Eidolon:** wields several powers at once but they're fading; terrified of becoming useless. His fear is more important than it looks.

### Scion
- The golden man, the first parahuman, drifts the world performing token rescues and saying almost nothing. The single most powerful being alive. The mystery of *what he is* and *why he does so little* is the campaign's final, world-ending answer. Handle with extreme restraint until the late arcs.

### Cauldron
- **Doctor Mother & Contessa:** a hidden organization that *sells* powers (with monstrous-mutation risks) and steers world events toward a secret goal they believe justifies any cost. **Contessa's** power — a flawless step-by-step "path" to any objective — makes her nearly omnipotent at small scales and is central to the endgame. Reveal slowly; their logic should feel awful and almost-reasonable.

## The Travelers
- **Trickster (Krouse):** Striker — swaps the positions of two things/people instantly. Devoted to Noelle to the point of damnation.
- **Noelle:** the team's tragic secret — a Cauldron experiment gone catastrophically wrong, hidden and contained by the others. Her unraveling is a major mid-late beat (the "Echidna" crisis). Keep her nature hidden until then.
- **Use:** outsiders pulled into the city's gravity; their loyalty and their secret drive a wrenching arc.
