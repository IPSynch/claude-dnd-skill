# House Rules & DM Operating Procedure — tomb-of-annihilation

**READ THIS AT EVERY `/dm:dnd load` and obey it for the whole campaign.** The player's standing instructions for how rolls and resources are handled.

---

## 1. Rolling — declare BEFORE the scene resolves, always show the math
- Before a scene's outcome hinges on a roll, **state what's being rolled and why**, then show the full breakdown in an OOC line, **then** narrate the result.
- **Format:** `(OOC rolling [Skill/check] to [purpose]: 1d20 (raw) + [modifier] + [other bonuses] = [Result] [total] vs [DC] DC!!)`
- **Always break out individual dice values** — never just totals.
- Examples:
  - `(OOC rolling Athletics to lift the gate: 1d20 (13) + 5 Athletics = 18 vs 18 DC — Success!)`
  - `(OOC rolling History to recall the gala's attendants: 1d20 (3) + 2 = Severe fail, 5 vs 16 DC — the noble draws a blank)`

## 2. Degrees of success/failure
- **Beat DC by +5** → notably better outcome. **By +10 (or nat 20)** → exceptional / critical-quality success, extra benefit.
- **Miss DC by 5** → notably worse. **By 10 (or nat 1)** → severe failure with real consequences.
- Let these shift the fiction, not just pass/fail.

## 3. Who I roll for — automatically, with the OOC breakdown
- **PCs:** skill checks, ability checks, saves, knowledge/recall, attacks — to **know or do something significant** — *as long as it spends no resource* (see §4). Show the OOC line.
- **NPCs / monsters:** **all** their rolls, using their statblock modifier. Show the OOC line. The dice decide what an NPC knows or pulls off; I don't just decide.

## 4. Resources — NEVER spend without the player's permission
- Do **NOT** expend, for any PC, without an explicit go-ahead: **spell slots** (any leveled spell), **limited-use features** (Channel Divinity, Lay on Hands, Innate Sorcery, once-per-rest item powers, etc.), **consumables / charges** (potions, scrolls, item charges).
- If a slotted spell or limited resource fits, **suggest it and wait**:
  `(OOC Suggestion: perfect spot for [spell] — a 3rd-level slot. Want to pull the plug?)`
- **Cantrips are FREE** — may be used automatically when fitting, damaging cantrips included when the scene and character clearly call for it.

## 5. Roll-handling flag
- `roll_mode: auto` — I roll and show the math — **plus** the §4 resource-permission gate. The player may flip to rolling their own PCs anytime.

## 6. ToA pacing note
- The **patron's decline** (Death Curse) and the **world death toll** are the campaign clock — dithering has visible cost. The jungle hexcrawl uses survival rolls (navigation/getting lost, foraging, disease) per §1's declared-and-shown format.
