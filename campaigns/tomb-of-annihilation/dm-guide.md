# DM Running Guide — tomb-of-annihilation
*Derived running toolkit, baked from the player's supplement ("A Guide to Tomb of Annihilation," Power Score RPG / Sean McGovern — personal-use). Summarized procedures and toggles, not a transcription. Pull exact page detail from the player's copy when a scene needs it.*

*Lazy reference — the **Opening Sequence** and **Hexcrawl Procedure** below are the parts to read early; the rest is pulled per-act.*

---

## DM Ethos (apply throughout)
- You're a **referee, not the players' enemy.** Don't take revenge for a circumvented encounter. You can kill them any time — that's not the job; making it fun is.
- **Not every encounter is hostile.** Seed good luck, helpful NPCs, and weird wonders alongside danger so the world doesn't feel uniformly out to get them.
- **Roll navigation (and other hidden checks) in secret** so a low roll the *player* sees doesn't become metagame knowledge the *character* shouldn't have.
- The toolkit is a sandbox — change anything. There's no wrong order.

## The Plot, Stripped to Its Spine
1. Heroes want to end the **death curse**. 2. They learn its source is the lost city of **Omu**. 3. They search the jungle for it. 4. They find it and gather the **nine puzzle cubes**. 5. They descend the **Tomb of the Nine Gods** and destroy the **Soulmonger** at the bottom.

## Opening Sequence — a strong Port Nyanzaru start
*(Adapted for 3 level-6 PCs; scale the pirates up.)*
1. **"We're on a boat."** The PCs arrive by ship (the *Brazen Pegasus*), a natural way to gather strangers — or they already know each other.
2. **Pirates!** The ship is boarded. Passengers flee; the PCs get a combat to show off. (Use a pirate captain/ship from the module — bump numbers/HP for a level-6 trio.) Optionally **Aremag the dragon turtle** surfaces to wreck the pirates — Port Nyanzaru's guardian.
3. **Arrival.** The harbormaster **Zindar** boards, is impressed, and sends them to a merchant prince with a writ.
4. **Meet a cursed prince.** Use **Jessamine** (a merchant prince who *has the death curse*) — meeting her makes the curse visceral and the clock real. The prince rewards the pirate-fight and sees potential in them.
5. **Dinosaur race.** The prince offers them mounts in the next race; winning funds the expedition. *(The supplement rates this the best set-piece in the book — lean in.)*
6. **Pick a guide.** They lodge at the **Thundering Lizard** or **Kaya's House** and audition guides (below). Then into the jungle.

**Why this works for our table:** at level 6 the PCs are credible enough that a merchant prince and Syndra recruiting them needs no excuse (resolves the "why hire nobodies?" problem). Syndra can be staying at **Wakanga O'tamu's** villa.

## Hexcrawl Procedure (Chapter 2 — run each travel day)
1. **Navigation check** (guide or PC). DC by terrain (per module). **Fail → party is lost**, ending in a random adjacent hex. *Roll this in secret.*
2. **Forage** — Survival **DC 10** (jungle is lush). Success: **d6 + WIS mod** lbs of food; roll again for gallons of water.
3. **Water** — each character needs **2 gallons/day**. A waterskin = ½ gallon (so ~4 each), or rely on **raincatchers** (useless if it doesn't rain). **No 2 gallons in a day → DC 15 CON save or 1 level of exhaustion.** River water must be **boiled** first. (Dehydration/forced-march/exhaustion: PH rules.)
4. **Encounters** — run any for the day; if none, narrate an uneventful leg and give them a free beat to do anything.
5. **Travel pace** — Walk **1 hex/day**; **Canoe 2 hexes/day**; **Hustle** d4 (3–4 → 2 hexes, −5 Perception); **Fly** (speed 30 → ~4 mi/hr).
6. **Camp** — first night, have them describe setup + watch order, then assume it repeats unless told otherwise (prevents retroactive "I was in a tree" after an ambush).

**Disease & rot** are part of the survival layer — fold in alongside water/exhaustion.

## Travel-Encounter Seed Bank (jungle days — mix good with bad)
*One-line seeds; expand on use. Sourced from the supplement's spread-out encounter days.*
- **Wukka nuts & the jaculi** — glowing nuts fall from wukka trees; a jaculi (camouflaged snake) lurks in the branches.
- **Brontosaurus stampede trick** — a harmless bronto herd grazes wildroot; further on, a zombie horde blocks the path. Clever parties **lure the herd with wildroot** to trample the zombies.
- **The zombie T-rex cache** — loot visible inside a "dead" tyrannosaur; climbing in wakes it (zombie T-rex).
- **"Have an ice day"** — frost giants (+ winter wolf) hunting **Artus Cimber** warn the party the Ring of Winter is corrupting — seeding doubt about Artus.
- **Honey-staked thief** — a man covered in honey, staked by goblins; freeing him reveals goblin zombie troubles.
- **Catapulted goblin village** — a "green meteor" in the sky is a goblin village launched right at the party.
- **Chwinga's gift** — a shy nature spirit watches, warns of a night zombie attack, then rewards the bravest with a **supernatural charm** (e.g. *charm of restoration* — gold for a healer-less party).
- **The chase** — Artus Cimber & Dragonbait sprint past fleeing a zombie T-rex; they won't stop to help.

## Guides — hooks (the one they pick seeds later threads)
- **Eku** (couatl polymorphed as a Chultan woman) — **knows where Omu is**; seeks heroes to go there; hates Nanny Pu'pu. *The "good" guide.*
- **Azaka Stormfang** (weretiger, hides it) — guides **free** if they fetch the *mask of the beast* from **Firefinger**; afraid of heights.
- **River Mist & Flask of Wine** (tabaxi sibling hunters, **Zhentarim**) — pay Jobal no fees; tasked to find Artus Cimber.
- **Hew Hackinstone** (berserker dwarf) — lost an arm to the red dragon **Tinder**; wants to kill it at **Wyrmheart Mine**.
- **Faroul & Gondolo** (human + halfling dandies) — broke gamblers with a flatulent triceratops named Zongo.
- **Qawasha & Kupalué** (Emerald Enclave druid + vegepygmy), **Musharib**, **Salida**, **Shago** — competent locals (see `npcs-full.md`).
- **Guild rules:** guides register under merchant prince **Jobal**; unregistered guides are "beaten, blinded, or beheaded." Standard fee ~5 gp/day, often 30 days up front (~150 gp).

## Reaching Omu (the route the supplement favors)
- **Kir Sabal** — aarakocra refuge; Princess **Mwaxanaré**; they can ritually grant **flight (speed 30, lasts 3 days)** — but it needs the **black orchid** from the ruins of **Nangalore**. Fetch orchid → return → fly toward Omu (magic runs out before arrival; finish on foot through undead-thick jungle).
- Omu "is notoriously hard to find... a basin hidden in the depths of the rainforest." Oracles (**Saja N'baza** at Orolunga; Kir Sabal) point the way.

## Omu — streamlined (supplement's simplification)
- Skip the heavy yuan-ti dungeon/grung sprawl: put the **yuan-ti, the dinosaur (King of Feathers), and the tabaxi hunters** in the **city itself**. The **hunters can tip the party to the shrines.**
- Party gathers the **9 puzzle cubes** through shrine puzzles while dealing with **yuan-ti ambushes and sudden rampages**.
- **Ras Nsi holds the last puzzle cube.** (The Red Wizards may hold cubes too.) Assembled cubes open the **Tomb of the Nine Gods**.

## The Tomb — running notes (pull trap detail from the player's copy)
- The Tomb's lethality is **traps and save-or-die puzzles**, not monster CR — so it stays dangerous for a level-6+ party.
- Examples of the flavor to expect (reference the module for exact DCs): permanent-blinding poison-needle traps, spiked pits, the **Withers** encounter (he quizzes then attacks; the party can recruit/avoid him), per-trickster-god **mask rituals** (e.g. Kubazan's froghemoth tomb), and **two legendary monsters** lurking off the main path (a **beholder** guarding a vault; an **aboleth** in an underground lake).
- **Acererak** rarely fights directly; the dungeon does the work. Destroying the **Soulmonger** in the **Cradle of the Death God** ends the curse.

## Table toggles for THIS campaign (3 × level 6, 2024 ruleset)
- **Scale up** pirate/jungle/Omu combats for a competent level-6 trio; the Tomb needs little scaling (trap-based).
- **Solo bosses** (Aremag, King of Feathers, Ras Nsi, legendary monsters, Acererak) get **+HP and legendary actions** if the double-turn house rule is in play — see `rules/combat-rules.md` once that's set.
- **Healer-less safety valves:** the chwinga *charm of restoration*, Cure Wounds on Limenra, and a guide-companion cover the missing cleric — important since the **death curse blocks resurrection**.
