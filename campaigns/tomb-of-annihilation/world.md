# World: tomb-of-annihilation
**Generated:** 2026-06-23  **Source:** *Tomb of Annihilation* (Wizards of the Coast, 2017) — imported as derived prep; full module text referenced from the player's own copy, not reproduced here.

## Campaign Tone & Genre
- **Tone:** dark fantasy / survival horror / pulp jungle adventure
- **Magic level:** medium (high among ancient ruins and the Tomb)
- **Setting type:** ancient/tropical — the peninsula of Chult, Forgotten Realms
- **Danger level:** lethal (death-trap dungeon; the adventure is explicitly about death)
- **Premise:** A soul-devouring curse is killing everyone the world over who has ever cheated death; the source lies in a death-trap tomb beneath a lost jungle city, and the party must find it before the curse claims them too.

## World Foundations

### Geography & Climate
- **Biome:** dense tropical rainforest, mangrove coast, volcanic highlands, river networks, ancient ruins swallowed by jungle.
- **Climate:** hot, wet, oppressive. Daily rain in the wet season; disease, heat exhaustion, and rot are constant threats. Travel is slow and navigation hard under unbroken canopy.
- **Notable features:** Port Nyanzaru (the only true city), the Aldani Basin, the River Tiryki and River Olung, Mount Fireburn and the volcanic plateau, the lost city of Omu in a sinkhole-ringed valley, countless overgrown ruins and dinosaur ranges.
- **Survival layer:** Chapter 2 is a hexcrawl. Track foraging, navigation (getting lost), disease, and random encounters. Guides hired in Port Nyanzaru mitigate getting lost.

### Magic System
- **Availability:** common in the city (priests, merchant-prince wealth, Red Wizard enclave); ancient and dangerous in the ruins.
- **Source:** divine (the trickster-god cult of old Omu; Chultan worship of Ubtao), arcane (Red Wizards, Harpers, wizard patrons), and the necromantic horror of Acererak.
- **Rules & constraints:** **The Death Curse is the defining magical fact.** Resurrection magic does not work while the curse holds — *raise dead, revivify, reincarnate, resurrection* all fail. Anyone previously raised from the dead is wasting away (see Threat below).
- **Wild magic / aberrations:** undead are unnaturally common in Chult; the Soulmonger's pull warps death itself across the world.

### Pantheon
- **Type:** polytheistic. Chultans revere **Ubtao** (creator, maze-keeper) chiefly; Port Nyanzaru's temples also serve Savras, Sune, Tymora, Waukeen, and others. The lost city of **Omu** worshipped **nine trickster gods** (see below) before Acererak destroyed them.
- **Active deities of note in play:**
  - **Ubtao** — Chultan creator-god; his protection over the dead has lapsed, which is why undead now plague the land.
  - **Savras** — divination; Grandfather Zitembe runs his temple in Port Nyanzaru.
  - **Sune** — love/beauty; relevant if a PC follows her.
  - **The Nine Trickster Gods of Omu** — see `## The Mystery` and `world-nodes.md`.

### Calendar
- **System:** Calendar of Harptos (Forgotten Realms). Faerûnian Year ~1490s DR.
- **Season:** the wet season is assumed at start (rain, mud, leeches). Adjust if the table prefers dry season.
- **In-world date:** set at first session (default: open in Port Nyanzaru, early in the wet season).

## The Settlement — Port Nyanzaru

A loud, hot, sun-bleached merchant city clinging to Chult's northern coast — the only safe harbor on a continent that wants everything dead. Ruled by seven **merchant princes**, freed from Amnian rule, defended by the dinosaur-mounted **Flaming Fist** mercenaries and its own laws. Smells of fish, fruit, dung, tar, and spice. Dinosaur races run through the streets for sport.

### Three Truths
- **Obvious:** It is the only civilized foothold in Chult and the staging ground for every expedition into the interior. Everything and everyone can be bought here.
- **Discoverable:** Each of the seven merchant princes has an agenda; outside factions (Harpers, Zhentarim, Order of the Gauntlet, Emerald Enclave, Red Wizards) all have agents working the party.
- **Secret:** Some who offer "help" serve the yuan-ti or the Red Wizards and want the party to lead them to the Tomb, not stop the curse.

### Settlement Details
- **Scale:** city  **Population:** ~30,000+
- **Wealth:** prosperous (merchant oligarchy; sharp wealth divide)
- **Law & Order:** 4 — order kept by the Flaming Fist and merchant-prince guards; harbor and gates strictly controlled.
- **Notable Districts & Locations:** Harbor & the Red Bazaar; Merchants' Ward (the princes' villas); Temple District (Savras, Sune, Tymora, Waukeen); Old City (poor, walled off, dinosaur pens); Grand Souk; Fort Beluarian (Flaming Fist garrison, just outside the city).

## The Nearby Threat — The Death Curse / the Soulmonger

A wasting curse grips the world: those who have ever been raised from the dead are rotting alive, losing hit points that no magic restores, and the dead can no longer be brought back at all. The cause is the **Soulmonger**, an artifact built by the archlich **Acererak** in the **Tomb of the Nine Gods** beneath Omu. It siphons souls to feed an **atropal** — a stillborn godling Acererak is nurturing toward divinity. Every soul the world loses feeds the thing.

### Three Truths
- **Obvious:** People who were once raised from the dead are dying and cannot be healed; resurrection magic has failed everywhere.
- **Discoverable:** The curse radiates from Chult; the trail leads through Port Nyanzaru → the jungle → the lost city of Omu.
- **Secret:** The Soulmonger and the atropal are Acererak's true design; the yuan-ti of the Fane have their own competing scheme to seize the Tomb's power.

## The Mystery — The Nine Trickster Gods & the Puzzle Cubes

Omu once worshipped nine **trickster gods**. Acererak slew the city, trapped the gods' spirits, and built his Tomb over their bones. To open the Tomb, the party must recover **nine puzzle cubes**, one from each god's shrine in the ruined city. The gods' spirits can aid (or hinder) those who solve their shrines.

### The Nine Trickster Gods (shrine reward = one puzzle cube each)
- **Obo'laka** — order/anxiety (zorbo)
- **Ijin** — change/chaos (flying monkey / almiraj motif)
- **Kubazan** — courage (froghemoth)
- **Moa** — loyalty (three-horned, jaculi)
- **Nangnang** — greed (grung)
- **Papazotl** — pride (eblis)
- **Shagambi** — wisdom/justice (kamadan)
- **Unkh** — indecision (flail snail/aurumvorax)
- **Wongo** — madness (su-monster)

### Clue Trail
- **Clue 1 (surface):** the patron (Syndra Silvane) and Port Nyanzaru sages know the curse comes from Chult's interior.
- **Clue 2 (investigation):** sages, the guides, and ruins (Orolunga's naga Saja N'baza) point toward the lost city of Omu.
- **Clue 3 (deep):** Omu's shrines hold the nine cubes; the cubes open the Tomb; the Tomb holds the Soulmonger.
- **Red herring:** the yuan-ti and Red Wizards offer "help" that serves their own claim on the Tomb.
- **Resolution condition:** destroy the Soulmonger and end the atropal.
- **Answer (DM only):** Acererak doesn't care about the curse — it's a side effect of farming souls for his atropal. He's largely absent until the final level, content to let intruders die in his traps.

## Factions

### The Merchant Princes of Port Nyanzaru — *trade oligarchy*
The seven rulers of the city. Wealthy, proud, rivalrous. They sponsor expeditions, dinosaur races, and side quests. See `npcs-full.md` for each (Ekene-Afa, Ifan Talro'a, Jessamine, Jobal, Kwayothé, Wakanga O'tamu, Zhanthi).
- **Attitude toward party:** neutral — courted as useful muscle.

### The Flaming Fist — *mercenary company / civic authority*
Mercenaries from Baldur's Gate garrisoning Fort Beluarian under **Liara Portyr**. They police the harbor and the road and have their own designs on Chult's wealth.
- **Attitude toward party:** neutral-suspicious.

### The Ytepka Society — *native resistance / civic guardians*
A secretive society of Chultans devoted to protecting the city's independence and traditions.
- **Attitude toward party:** neutral.

### Outside Factions (each fields agents who recruit the party)
- **Harpers** — want the curse stopped, the Soulmonger destroyed. Generally allied. (Agent example: Eku.)
- **Order of the Gauntlet** — righteous; want the undead threat purged.
- **Emerald Enclave** — protect Chult's wilds; want balance restored. (Agent example: Qawasha.)
- **Lords' Alliance / Flaming Fist** — political and mercantile gain.
- **Zhentarim** — profit and leverage; will betray if it pays.
- **Red Wizards of Thay** — **antagonists.** Servants of **Valindra Shadowmantle** (Szass Tam's lich lieutenant) want the Soulmonger/atropal for themselves. Some "patrons" and agents are theirs.
- **The Yuan-ti of the Fane (Cult of the Night Serpent)** — **antagonists.** Under **Ras Nsi** and the nightmare speaker **Fenthaza**, they hold Omu and the Tomb's threshold and want to claim its power.
